Zero-Kanban

Zero-Kanban (0KB) is built for small, elite teams (max 4) tasked with rapidly building and launching a single project under skunkworks conditions.

It prioritises speed, autonomy, and minimal overhead, breaking free from traditional processes while maintaining razor-sharp focus.

Diff from Classic Kanban

  1. Limit Workflow Stages
    • Traditional Kanban: Multiple columns like "To Do," "In Progress," "Review," "Done."
    • Zero-Kanban: Only three: Staged, Active, Won. Simplicity supports focus and speed.
  2. Strict WIP Limits
    • Traditional Kanban: WIP limits exist but are flexible across teams.
    • Zero-Kanban: A hard cap on Active tasks per person (e.g., 1-2). Keeps focus tight and prevents overloading.
  3. Single Mission Focus
    • Traditional Kanban: Boards can track multiple workflows or projects.
    • Zero-Kanban: Each board is dedicated to a single mission. No distractions or parallel tracks.
  4. Binary Estimation
    • Traditional Kanban: Uses detailed effort estimation methods like story points or time estimates, often requiring team discussions and granular values.

      • Light: Quick wins or simple tasks (~1 day or less)
      • Heavy: More complex or multi-step objectives. (> 1 day)
